# Dividend Policy Introduction

A dividend is a cash distribution from a corporation to its shareholders.
Boards declare dividends out of available retained earnings subject to
state corporate law.

## Key Dates

The declaration date is when the board approves the dividend. The
ex-dividend date is when the shares trade without the right to the upcoming
payment. The record date determines the shareholders of record. The payable
date is when cash settles to the holder.

## Payout Considerations

Mature businesses with stable cash generation often adopt a fixed or
progressive dividend policy. Growth-stage businesses typically reinvest
earnings rather than distribute them.
