# US Initial Public Offering Process

A US initial public offering converts a private company into a publicly
traded one. The issuer files an S-1 registration statement with the
Securities and Exchange Commission and works with underwriting banks to
price the offering.

## Roadshow

Management presents the business to institutional investors during a
roadshow that typically spans two weeks. The bookrunners build an order
book and recommend a price within the indicated range.

## Pricing and Allocation

Final pricing occurs the evening before the trading debut. The lead
underwriter allocates shares across the order book and provides a stabilizing
bid in the open market for 30 days following the offering.
