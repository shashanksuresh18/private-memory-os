# Securities Lending Basics

Securities lending is the temporary transfer of equities or bonds from a
lender to a borrower against collateral. The borrower pays a lending fee
and returns equivalent securities at the end of the loan.

## Use Cases

Short sellers borrow shares to deliver against a short sale. Market makers
borrow to cover settlement obligations. Index funds and pension plans
typically lend out passive holdings to capture fee income.

## Collateral

Cash collateral programs reinvest the cash into short-term liquidity vehicles.
Non-cash collateral programs accept high-grade government securities or
equity baskets, subject to daily mark-to-market and haircut requirements.
