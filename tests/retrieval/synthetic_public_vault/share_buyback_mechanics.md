# Share Buyback Mechanics

A share buyback is a corporate action through which a company repurchases
its own outstanding shares. Buybacks reduce share count and can support
earnings per share metrics.

## Open Market Repurchases

Most buybacks execute through ordinary open-market trades subject to the
Rule 10b-18 safe harbor under the Securities Exchange Act of 1934. The rule
sets daily volume, timing, and price conditions to limit market impact.

## Accelerated Share Repurchases

In an accelerated share repurchase, the company contracts with an
investment bank to borrow shares and deliver them immediately. The bank
covers the borrow over a defined averaging period at the volume-weighted
average price.
