# Proxy Voting Overview

Proxy voting is the mechanism through which shareholders cast their votes
at corporate meetings without attending in person. Public companies
distribute annual proxy materials describing the matters submitted to a
vote.

## Annual Meeting Items

Common ballot items include the election of directors, ratification of the
independent auditor, advisory votes on executive compensation, and the
adoption or amendment of equity compensation plans. Shareholder proposals
submitted under Rule 14a-8 may also appear.

## Vote Tabulation

An independent inspector of elections tabulates the votes and certifies the
result. Most matters require a simple majority of votes cast; certain
governance matters require supermajority or class-specific approvals.
