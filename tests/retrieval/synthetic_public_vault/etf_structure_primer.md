# Exchange-Traded Fund Structure

An exchange-traded fund is a pooled investment vehicle whose shares trade
intraday on a stock exchange. Most ETFs are registered under the Investment
Company Act of 1940.

## Creation and Redemption

Authorized participants create and redeem ETF shares in large blocks called
creation units. The in-kind creation mechanism keeps the ETF market price
close to the net asset value of the underlying basket.

## Tax Treatment

In-kind redemptions allow ETFs to externalize unrealized gains, which often
gives equity ETFs a lower distribution profile than comparable mutual funds.
Investors still owe capital gains taxes upon sale of their ETF shares.
