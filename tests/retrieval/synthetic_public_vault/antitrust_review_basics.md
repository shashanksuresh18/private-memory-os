# US Antitrust Review for Mergers

Large mergers and acquisitions in the United States require pre-closing
review under the Hart-Scott-Rodino Antitrust Improvements Act. Parties file
notifications with the Federal Trade Commission and the Department of
Justice Antitrust Division.

## Waiting Period

The standard initial waiting period is 30 days. Either agency may issue a
second request for additional information, which extends the period until
substantial compliance plus an additional waiting interval.

## Substantive Standards

Reviewers apply the Clayton Act standard of substantially lessening
competition. Recent merger guidelines emphasize labor market effects,
vertical theories, and platform dynamics alongside traditional horizontal
analysis.
